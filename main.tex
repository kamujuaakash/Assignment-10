  In a two-dimensional random walk.The coordinates x(t) and y(t) of a moving object are two independent random-walk processes with the same s and T.Given a $z(t) = \sqrt{x^2(t) + y^2(t)}$\\

   \end{block}

    \begin{frame}{Question Continued}
        \begin{section}{Question Continued}
            \begin{block}{Question Continued}
            Show that if $z(t) = \sqrt{x^2(t) + y^2(t)}$ is the distance of the object from the origin and $t >> T$, then for z of the order of $\sqrt{\alpha t}$ :  
$$f_z(z,t) \simeq \frac{z}{\alpha t} e^{\frac{-z^2}{2 \alpha t}}U(z)$$
where $\alpha = \frac{S^2}{t}$
        \end{block}
       \end{section}
  \end{frame}
  \begin{frame}
        \begin{section}{Solution}
            \begin{block}{Solution}
            For large t, x(t) and y(t) can be approximated by two independent Wiener processes as in 
                     
$$f_x(x,t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \alpha t}} e^{\frac{-x^2}{2 \alpha t}} $$$$f_y(y,t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \alpha t}} e^{\frac{-y^2}{2 \alpha t}}$$
Hence z(t) has a Rayleigh density.The product $f_z(z,t)dz$ equals approximately the probability z(t) is between z and z + dz provided that dz $> >$ T. 
        \end{block}
       \end{section}
  \end{frame}
  \begin{frame}
        \begin{section}{Solution Continued}
            \begin{block}{Solution Continued}
            Here z(t) is a discrete type RV taking the values$\sqrt{m^2 + n^2}$ where m,n are integers.\\
            Hence that if $z(t) = \sqrt{x^2(t) + y^2(t)}$ is the distance of the object from the origin and $t >> T$, then z is the order of $\sqrt{\alpha t}$ 
            and
$$f_z(z,t) \simeq \frac{z}{\alpha t} e^{\frac{-z^2}{2 \alpha t}}U(z)$$
where $\alpha = \frac{S^2}{t}$ 
        \end{block}
